01 — 10

the girl with notes and a boy with carrom

July 2023 · Tagit interview room

It all started in July 2023. I was about to attend my first interview at Tagit — the very first interview of my life. No nervousness, nothing. Cleared the rounds easily. Then they moved us to a separate room — 5 people were there, including my girl. Haha, but at that time she was just a girl who came for the interview. At that moment I had no idea she would become my life. But life is always full of unexpected moments, right? It proved exactly that. She was preparing for the next round with notes in her hand — it felt like a girl studying for board exams. Everyone else in the room was doing the same thing, while I was sitting there playing carrom on my phone. Do you know who that girl is? I'll tell you in the next chapter.

02 — 10

a hi, a smile and a name I'll never forget

Day 1 at Tagit · the seat God arranged

Interview done, got the job at Tagit. First day of our corporate life — excited and at the same time the pressure was there. Can we survive here? Can we have friends here? But the good thing is everyone came out of college so we had a training session for almost 1 month and it felt just like college. Tagit is the company that gave me two lives — one for my professional career, the kickstart of everything, and another, a new beginning of something I never knew was coming. That beginning came when she herself sat next to me. Haha. I think the seat arrangements were not done by management — they were done by GOD. Because why would a service team girl come and sit next to a product team guy? She could have sat somewhere else, right? If she hadn't sat next to me, I don't think I would be creating this story today. I said hi to her and she replied with a hi and a smile. She used to talk with a lot of formality at first — I told her she can talk casually, no need to be formal. I forgot to mention one thing — the beautiful name of the girl. Her name is Surekaa. The name that is going to be etched in my heart for the rest of my life — though at that time I had no idea.

03 — 10

fifteen days like a tracer bullet

rose milk, Java patterns & tears at the door

Fifteen days went like a tracer bullet — so fast. I was going to the product team (MB side) and she to the service side. Moving out of that training room, after so many years I felt bad about moving away from someone. It was weird, actually. Even I was in a relationship at the time — actually, both of us were. But the bond we had during those 15 days — we used to tease each other, oh sorry, I used to tease her, she used to smile and say "hey, poda" always with a sportive mindset. We'd go drink rose milks. We'd discuss Java patterns that I've never once used in my professional career. Unlimited laughter inside that room. Everything hit me when I came out of that room. It's somehow special, right — if a girl you've known for just 15 days has tears in her eyes when you're leaving, and you feel exactly the same. That day a new bond was created — not in the form of a relationship, but in the form of friends. I don't know what she felt. But it was something special to me, obviously. And the boy moved out — his name is Aswath. Yes, that's me, as you know.

04 — 10

the bond that never faded

8 months apart · the trip · possessiveness

Then the break came — both Surekaa and I separated to different teams. The pressure on freshers led us to talk less. Since this was the first company for both of us, we focused on learning new things. That's how 8 months went. But somehow a gang was formed and we came back to our routine. And the beautiful thing is — even though we had fewer conversations during this period, the bond never faded. That's how we became close friends. The trip made us even closer — closer than this boy had ever been with anyone. A bond he never had with anyone. A bond that feels like something you cannot get from anyone else, not after her. So I was always there for her and she was always there for me. This was the first time I experienced possessiveness inside a friendship. She did the same — possessive over silly things. Always prioritising each other in all situations. She used to sit next to me every single day for lunch. She would always be around me — no matter what — she liked being around me. Something like that doesn't happen on purpose, right? It just happens eventually. And it happened. One day she wore my hoodie to the theatre and we clicked a picture together. The first thing we did after becoming close friends was clicking thousands of pictures wherever we went. Whoever saw those pictures said one thing only: "you look so cute together." Of course, even without me she looks cute — but cute together is something else, right? Which is why we kept taking thousands of pictures that are still in my drive — ones I cannot erase, even when Google removed the access.

05 — 10

two helmets one reserved for her

every Wednesday · our day

After the trip, the bond we had went to another level. Next level in the sense that it's not a relationship — we were like best friends who always stuck together. Wednesday was our day, because Thursday and Friday were WFH for both of us, so we always went out somewhere on Wednesday evening. It reached a point where I was waiting for Wednesday all week. That day I'd take my bike from my room and take two helmets with me — one on my head and another one in the bike dickey. Single earbud on my left and keeping another one in the box — the one reserved for her. Then I'd reach her PG. Lucky smile, I think. Once I arrived I'd call her, she'd check from the first-floor balcony to confirm if I'd actually come. After confirming, she'd come down. And we'd start the bike ride with songs on. It felt like the mental manadhil song from OK Kanmani — that exact feeling. We'd have so much fun, actually singing our favourite songs together. Oh, I forgot to tell — we had one song that was like a signature song for us: Thooya. We used to listen to it whenever we were on a bike ride. It was so much fun and so heartfelt, actually.

06 — 10

thooya on the speakers sand under our feet

Thiruvanmiyur · parotta, kalakki & beach

Then we'd reach the beach — Thiruvanmiyur beach. We'd move around the sands with the peaceful sound of the sea. But before going there we'd always stop at the local kai yendhi bhavan. We used to eat parotta, dosa, and kalakki — often, every time we visited we'd have it. She'd feed me some days, I'd feed her. It's a cute thing, right? Then we'd go to the beach, drink from the flask there, take more buttermilk with us to the beach and sit there talking about useless things. I still remember the day she posted a snap of just a cup — and behind that, me wearing a green striped tee. She never thought about what people would think of her for roaming around with a boy at night and on the beach. That's Surekaa — if it's right for her, she doesn't care what others think. That's a pretty good quality, right? Yes it is. We'd eat waffles sometimes. We'd drink coffee. We'd visit Tea Talkies. And we'd visit this one hotel — oh god, I forgot the name — the hotel inside Karappakkam. Let's see if she reminds me the name. Let her tell the hotel name. We used to eat parotta and kalakki there as always, and Idly Vada Kadai's elaneer payasam — that I will never forget — and Tiffin Shastra's rava dosa. She always knew what I'd eat even before I told her. Haha.

07 — 10

she took my mother's hand

Bommi Apartments · paal kaachanum · Tiffin Shastra

Days went by. I was living in a room with friends and trying to move to an individual house. I don't know why, but I always kept Surekaa in my mind when I thought about it — if I rent a house I can invite her and spend quality time, right? That thought would never have come to my mind if it wasn't for Surekaa, because I am not the guy who has the courage to bring a girl to my house. But somehow in my mind I thought I can bring her over, watch movies together, something like that. I don't know why. Reason? IDK. And that's the truth — for some things we don't need a reason. Something is beyond that. So I got a house on rent and it was time to start living there. I thought I would live there alone. But you don't know what happened next — and it surprised me as well. If you asked me now in 2026 would you believe this could have happened back then, I honestly don't think I would believe it. But it happened. It's kind of the beginning of something — and the beginning of fulfilling the reason I was born, I think.

We had to do paal kaachanum — which we usually do at the best time, early morning at 5 o'clock. Before that day, my parents came from Thiruvarur and stayed at the new home. That same evening, there was a plan — my mother and I had to go buy things for the next morning ritual. Unfortunately I got stuck with office work. The first thing that came to my mind was her. I took my phone and called her. She said she was also occupied with office work, then she called again and said "nan poren, konjam late aagum" — she might have thought, Aswath is asking, how can I not go? She arrived at my home and took my mom out to buy everything that was needed. She is the first girl I ever introduced to my mother. They shared such a good bond that day. My mom said her favourite colour is blue — same as Surekaa's. Just saying. Then me, Surekaa, Ishu and Amma had breakfast at Tiffin Shastra. Then my parents went back to Thiruvarur.

08 — 10

she slept next to me like she always belonged there

our home · blue bedsheet · Bommi Apartments

After some days, the AC got fixed — and the home became messy and sandy after all the drilling. I still don't know how I had the courage to ask a girl for help with this and also invite her to stay with me. The trust she had in me — she came and helped me. CSK match was being telecasted. She even clicked a picture of me with a mop in my hand. Cleaning done. And we had to sleep, right? She never hesitated. She just slept next to me without the fear of being with a boy at night in an individual home. It made me feel so special. She slept like a child. She used the blue bedsheet — which later became our favourite. That's a different story, haha. I even took a picture of her while she was sleeping because it felt so cute and it surprised me — to see a girl next to me who wasn't my girlfriend (till that point). Then we used to spend Thursday to Sunday in our new rented home. Yes — our home. Not my home. Because somehow she became part of it.

09 — 10

Jersey made me cry she made it worthwhile

AGS Navalur · ezhaneer nungu · the nights we had

We used to watch movies at night. The first movie was Jersey — literally on my bucket list to watch it with her. Usually whenever I see Jersey my eyes fill with tears, and it was the same that day. But I still remember what she said after we became a couple — it was weird when the two characters started kissing on screen. Haha, I felt the same, I told her. Then we used to watch horror movies and she'd close her eyes and come closer to me to feel safe. Those touches were not filled with anything except warmth and trust. Those feelings cannot be expressed in words. Then we went to the food court to get our favourite ezhaneer nungu. And we went to so many movies at AGS Navalur. But till this point we're friends — close friends, maybe more than that. Then one incident changed everything — turned my life in the opposite direction. Probably the best thing that has happened in my life. If you're reading this Surekaa, you already know what happened next.

Bommi Apartments · thousands of kisses · forever

Then what happened — you know, I know. Our home knows. Our room knows. Our blue bedsheet knows. Our bathrooms know. Our hall knows. Our beanbag knows. Our kitchen knows. Our sofa knows. Our balcony knows. Our terrace knows. Bommi Apartments knows everything — what we had, what bond we shared, what privacy we shared. We don't even have a count of how many times we kissed. I hope the apartment and all its bricks know it too — maybe they also thought, when will these two stop? Haha. We used to sleep next to each other. I'd put my hands on you easily without hesitation, and you too. One day we started playing the neck biting game — THE GAME — the one that made us both finally say what we had for each other. To make your lips wet I kissed you and you closed your eyes and went under my arms. That felt so cute. After that, the only things we did were kissing kissing kissing, cuddling cuddling. Then we started removing our clothes — we rarely put clothes on when we slept. There was always that closeness in the nights. All our nights were like that — talking, being close to you, kissing your neck, feeling the warmth of you, sticking together like glue, feeling the heat of our bodies without any distance between us. Oh my god, the intimacy we had was something truly great. Then we expressed our love. The love came first — not the other way around. Love was always there, we just hadn't expressed it yet. And then you know how we became — thousands of photos, thousands of kisses, and more memories than we can count. You used to visit my home and my mom loved you. My family was ready to give their son to you, haha. Yes — after that, a lot happened. You went to CBE and I went to Tringapps. The problems started after our families found out about our love story. I will never forget the way you stood for this love. What you did is not something easily done. No matter what, no matter what happened, you were always there — standing strong and tall — proving that a woman can love a man like this. I don't know if I'm perfect or not. But you're perfect in each and everything. Still, for almost a year now these problems have been going on like a never-ending story. But soon we will sort everything out and I will marry you with everyone's blessings. I will be there for you till the very last breath of my life. Love you Surekaa, as always. Love you kiddoo.
