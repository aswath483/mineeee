01 — 08

the girl with notes and a boy with carrom

July 2023 · Tagit interview room

It all started in July 2023. First interview, first shot at the corporate world — and somehow, not a single nerve. I cleared the rounds easily. Then they moved us to a waiting room — five of us. That's where I first saw her. She had notes in her hand, studying for the next round like a girl before board exams. Everyone else in the room was doing the same. Me? I was playing carrom on my phone. I had no idea the girl sitting across from me was about to change everything.

02 — 08

a hi, a smile and a name I'll never forget

Day 1 at Tagit · the seat God arranged

We got the job. First day at Tagit — excitement, a little pressure, the usual nerves. Everyone was fresh out of college, so training felt just like it. Then something happened I can only explain one way: God arranged the seating. Why would a service team girl sit next to a product team guy? There's no reason. But she did. I said hi. She replied with a smile. She spoke very formally at first — I told her she didn't need to. That's when I learned her name. Surekaa. A name I didn't know yet would be etched in my heart forever. Fifteen days flew like a tracer bullet — rose milks, Java pattern discussions, unlimited laughter. When we had to move to separate teams, I felt something I hadn't felt in years: bad about leaving someone. She had tears in her eyes at the door. I felt the same. A new bond had begun — not a relationship, but something just as real.

03 — 08

fifteen days like a tracer bullet

rose milk, Java patterns & tears at the door

Then came the separation. Different teams, the pressure of being freshers — we focused on learning and talked less. Eight months passed. But somehow a gang formed and we found our way back. The beautiful thing? The bond never faded — not even a little. A trip made us even closer. Closer than I'd ever been with anyone. I'd never felt possessiveness inside a friendship before, but there it was — both ways. She always found the seat next to mine at lunch. She was always nearby, like she just liked being around me. One day she wore my hoodie to the theatre. Whoever saw our photos said the same thing every time: you two look so cute together.

04 — 08

two helmets one reserved for her

every Wednesday · our day

Wednesday was our day. Thursday and Friday were WFH, so every Wednesday evening belonged to us. I'd leave my room with two helmets — one on my head, one in the box, reserved only for her. One earbud in my left ear, the other waiting. I'd reach her PG and call. She'd lean over the first-floor balcony to confirm I was actually there — then come down. And we'd ride off with songs playing. Our signature song was Thooya. Every bike ride, every time it played, everything felt like a scene from OK Kanmani. I started living for Wednesdays.

05 — 08

thooya on the speakers sand under our feet

Thiruvanmiyur · parotta, kalakki & beach

We'd stop at the kai yendhi bhavan first — parotta, dosa, kalakki, always. Some days she'd feed me, some days I'd feed her. Then to Thiruvanmiyur beach. Walking on the sand with the sound of the sea all around us. She once snapped a photo of just a tea cup — and behind it, me in a green striped tee. She never once worried about what people would think, a girl roaming the beach at night with a boy. That's Surekaa — if something feels right to her, the world doesn't matter. Waffles, coffee, Tea Talkies, that one hotel in Karappakkam where we always ordered the same things. She always knew what I'd order before I even opened my mouth.

06 — 08

she slept next to me like she always belonged there

our home · blue bedsheet · Bommi Apartments

I got a rented house. For the first time in my life, I thought about inviting a girl home — and that thought only ever came with Surekaa. I can't explain why. Some things don't need a reason. My parents came one evening for a paal kaachanum ritual. I got stuck with office work. First person I thought of: her. She came, took my mom out to get everything we needed. She was the first girl I ever introduced to my mother. They clicked instantly — my mom's favourite colour is blue, and so is Surekaa's. Weeks later, AC drilling left the place a mess. She came to help without hesitation. CSK match on TV, mop in my hand, her clicking a photo of it all. After the cleaning, she stayed. She slept next to me that night — on the blue bedsheet — like a child, without a single fear. I took a photo of her sleeping because I couldn't believe it. Thursday to Sunday became ours. It stopped being my home. It became ours.

07 — 08

Jersey made me cry she made it worthwhile

AGS Navalur · ezhaneer nungu · the nights we had

Jersey had been on my bucket list for years — I always wanted to watch it with her. When the emotional scenes came, my eyes filled just like always. Horror movies were a different story — she'd close her eyes and come closer, holding on. Those touches weren't about anything except just being there for each other. We'd go to the food court for ezhaneer nungu. Countless evenings at AGS Navalur. Through all of it, we were friends. Close friends. Maybe something more — but we hadn't said it yet. Then one evening, something small happened. And it changed everything.

08 — 08

love was always there we just finally said it

Bommi Apartments · thousands of kisses · forever

What happened next — you know, I know. Our home knows. The blue bedsheet knows. Every wall of Bommi Apartments holds it. The game that said everything without words. The kiss that made it real. After that it was just us — thousands of photos, thousands of moments, a love that was always there, finally spoken out loud. You came home, my mom loved you, my family was ready to give their son to you. Then you moved to Coimbatore. I moved to Tringapps. Problems came when our families found out. But I will never forget the way you stood for this love — without flinching, without running. No matter what, you were always there. I don't know if I'm perfect. But you are, at everything. We'll sort this out. And one day soon, I'll marry you with everyone's blessings. Love you, Surekaa. Always. LY KY FY.
